🌬️ Respiration 呼吸作用

The chemical reactions inside cells that break down nutrient molecules to release energy for metabolism.

Most organisms use oxygen (aerobic respiration 有氧呼吸). Some can do it without oxygen (anaerobic respiration 无氧呼吸).

Key Point: Respiration ≠ breathing! It's a cellular process happening in every living cell.

🔔 Irritability (Sensitivity) 应激性

The ability to detect changes in the internal or external environment and respond appropriately.

Examples: A plant growing towards light, you pulling your hand away from a hot surface, bacteria moving away from harmful chemicals.

🍽️ Nutrition (Nourish) 营养

Taking in materials for energy, growth, and development. Different organisms have different methods:

• Plants: Photosynthesis 光合作用 (make own food)
• Animals: Ingest other organisms
• Fungi: Absorb nutrients from dead matter

📈 Growth & Development 生长与发育

Growth: Permanent increase in size and dry mass （干重） (not just water absorption!). Caused by increase in cell number or cell size.

Development: Changes in form and function as an organism matures (e.g., tadpole → frog).

🗑️ Excretion 排泄

Removal of waste products of metabolism （代谢废物） (toxic substances produced by chemical reactions in cells).

In humans: CO₂ (from lungs), urea (from kidneys), sweat. In plants: O₂ (at night), some compounds via leaves.

Note: Excretion ≠ egestion （排遗：排出未消化的食物残渣）. They're different!

🧬 Reproduction 繁殖

Processes that make more of the same kind of organism — producing new individuals.

Two types:
• Asexual 无性繁殖: One parent only (e.g., binary fission, budding)
• Sexual 有性繁殖: Two parents, involves fertilization (offspring vary from parents)

🔗
How These Characteristics Connect
Living things need energy to carry out all life processes. Energy comes from nutrition (food), is released through respiration, and powers everything else — growth, movement, response, reproduction, and excretion. Without energy input, none of these processes are possible!
🧪
The Dawn of Life — Primordial Soup Theory
Scientists believe Earth formed ~5 billion years ago. The atmosphere had no oxygen. About 3.8–2.8 billion years ago, the first simple living organisms appeared in the "primordial soup" — shallow seas containing molecules that could combine into complex structures. This is where life began!
②
Classification — Why & How? 分类学
Organising the diversity of life into meaningful groups.
❓
Why Do We Need Classification?
Three main reasons:
1. Conservation 保护: Identify which species need protection in their natural habitats.
2. Breeding programmes 繁育计划: Manage which organisms are used in controlled breeding.
3. Evolutionary relationships 进化关系: Understand how organisms are related by common ancestry.
🔑 Classification Keys (Dichotomous Keys) 检索表

A tool used by taxonomists （分类学家） (scientists who classify organisms) to identify unknown organisms by asking a series of yes/no questions, each with two possible answers.

How a dichotomous key works:
Each question splits the possibilities into two branches. You follow the path based on your observations until you reach the organism's identity. Think of it like a "choose-your-own-adventure" book for biology!

Traditional classification keys were based on morphology 形态学 (external shape/form) and anatomy 解剖学 (internal structure) — features scientists could easily observe and measure.

Modern classification also uses DNA/protein analysis: organisms that share very similar DNA sequences are closely related. For example, humans and chimpanzees share ~98.6% of their genes!

Pro tip: A classification key is called "dichotomous" because "di-" means two and "-tomous" means cutting — each step cuts the options in half (YES or NO).

The Five Kingdoms of Life 五界系统
All living organisms belong to one of these five major groups.
Kingdom Cell Type Key Features Examples
Prokaryote
原核生物		 No nucleus （无细胞核）
(no membrane-bound organelles)		 Unicellular 单细胞, very small, DNA in a circular strand (not in a nucleus). No mitochondria, chloroplasts, or Golgi apparatus. Bacteria (e.g., Salmonella)
Protoctist
原生生物		 Nucleus present
Can be unicellular OR multicellular		 Very diverse group. Some are animal-like (heterotrophs 异养), others plant-like (photosynthetic 光合自养). Don't fit neatly into other kingdoms. Amoeba, Paramecium, Seaweed (Plasmodium)
Fungi
真菌		 Eukaryotic cells with nuclei Heterotrophic feeders (absorb nutrients from dead/decaying organic matter). Cell walls made of chitin （几丁质） (NOT cellulose). Most are multicellular (hyphae/mycelium structure). Mushrooms, Mould, Yeast, Penicillium
Plant
植物		 Eukaryotic cells with nuclei & chloroplasts Multicellular, autotrophic （自养） (photosynthesis). Cell walls made of cellulose （纤维素）. Contains chlorophyll for photosynthesis. Trees, Flowers, Mosses, Ferns, Algae*
Animal
动物		 Eukaryotic cells with nuclei (NO cell wall) Multicellular, heterotrophic （异养） (ingest food). No cell walls, no chloroplasts. Usually capable of movement. Humans, Insects, Fish, Birds, Worms
⚠️
Important Distinctions to Remember
• Fungi vs Plants: Fungi have chitin cell walls + absorb food. Plants have cellulose cell walls + make their own food via photosynthesis.
• Prokaryote vs Protoctist: Prokaryotes have NO nucleus. Protoctists DO have a nucleus.
• *Algae note: Some algae are classified as Protoctists (unicellular ones), while multicellular algae may be grouped with plants depending on the system.
Quick Decision Tree for Kingdoms:
Does it have a nucleus? → NO = Prokaryote | YES → continue
Is it multicellular? → NO = likely Protoctist (or unicellular fungus) | YES → continue
Does it make its own food? → YES = Plant | NO → Animal or Fungi
Does it absorb nutrients externally? → YES = Fungi | NO = Animal

Hierarchy of Classification 分类层级
From broadest group to most specific — the 7 levels of biological classification.

The 7 Taxonomic Levels (Broad → Specific):

Kingdom 界 → Phylum 门 → Class 纲 → Order 目 → Family 科 → Genus 属 → Species 种

💡 Moving DOWN the hierarchy → organisms share MORE similarities but FEWER differences between members.

🦁 Binomial Nomenclature (Two-Name System) 双名法

Every species has a unique two-part scientific name in Latin. This system was developed by Swedish scientist Carl Linnaeus （林奈）.

Example: Lion's scientific name

Panthera leo

Panthera = Genus name (capitalised) leo = Species name (lowercase, italicised)

Rules for writing scientific names:

  1. Genus name always capitalised; species name always lowercase
  2. Both names must be italicised (or underlined if handwritten)
  3. Both parts together uniquely identify ONE species worldwide
  4. The genus groups closely related species together

More examples:

  • Human: Homo sapiens
  • Dog (domestic): Canis lupus familiaris
  • House cat: Felis catus
Memory trick: "King Philip Came Over For Good Soup" = Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species 🍲

Vertebrate Animals — Five Classes 脊椎动物五纲
Animals with backbones, divided into five classes based on adaptations.
🦴
What is a Vertebrate? （什么是脊椎动物？）
A vertebrate is an animal belonging to the phylum Chordata （脊索动物门） that has a hard, bony internal skeleton made of bones called vertebrae （脊椎骨） (backbone). This allows them to move with greater speed and strength than invertebrates. All vertebrates are animals (Animal Kingdom).
🐟

Fish 鱼类

~35,000+ species
  • Cold-blooded 变温/冷血 (ectothermic)
  • Breath using gills 鳃
  • Body covered in scales 鳞片 (often with mucus)
  • Have fins 鳍 for movement and stability
  • Lateral line detects vibrations in water
  • Most lay eggs in water (external fertilisation 体外受精)
  • Examples: Salmon, Shark, Goldfish, Stickleback
🐸

Amphibians 两栖动物

~8,000+ species
  • Cold-blooded (ectothermic)
  • Double life: larva in water, adult on land
  • Skin is moist and smooth (no scales)
  • Breathe via lungs AND skin (cutaneous respiration 皮肤呼吸)
  • Adults have four limbs (webbed feet)
  • Undergo metamorphosis 变态发育 (tadpole → frog)
  • Most lay eggs in water (no shell)
  • Examples: Frogs, Toads, Salamanders, Newts
🦎

Reptiles 爬行动物

~11,000+ species
  • Cold-blooded (ectothermic)
  • Skin covered in dry scales (prevents water loss)
  • Breathe using lungs only
  • Most have four limbs (some like snakes lost them)
  • Lay eggs on land with leathery shells （革质卵壳） (or give live birth)
  • Well-developed lungs from hatching
  • Examples: Lizards, Snakes, Turtles, Crocodiles
🦅

Birds 鸟类

~10,000+ species
  • Warm-blooded 恒温/温血 (endothermic)
  • Body covered in feathers 羽毛 (for flight & insulation)
  • Forelimbs modified into wings 翅膀
  • Beak 喙 (no teeth!) adapted to diet type
  • Breathe through efficient lungs
  • Hollow bones （中空骨骼） (reduce weight for flight)
  • Lay hard-shelled eggs
  • Examples: Eagle, Penguin, Sparrow, Ostrich, Heron
🦁

Mammals 哺乳动物

~6,400+ species
  • Warm-blooded (endothermic)
  • Body covered in fur/hair 毛发 (insulation)
  • Females have mammary glands 乳腺 produce milk
  • Young usually develop inside mother (most give live birth 胎生)
  • Differentiated teeth （异型齿） (incisors, canines, molars)
  • Highly developed brain
  • Diaphragm muscle for breathing
  • Examples: Humans, Dogs, Whales, Bats, Lions
Vertebrate Decision Key (Quick Version):
Smooth moist skin? → Amphibian (NO) ↓
Has scales?
　├ Dry scales? → Reptile (NO) ↓
　├ Scales + lives in water? → Fish (NO) ↓
Has feathers? → Bird (NO) → Mammal (has fur/hair + mammary glands)

🧠
Humans as Mammals — Special Adaptations
Humans show all typical mammalian traits PLUS unique adaptations:
• Opposable thumbs 对生拇指 → precise grip for tool use
• Bipedalism 双足行走 → walking upright frees hands
• Large, complex brain 发达的大脑 → language, abstract thinking
• Binocular vision 双目视觉 → depth perception
• Versatile diet 杂食 → omnivorous (can eat almost anything!)

Viruses — Alive or Not? 病毒
The edge case that doesn't fit into ANY of the five kingdoms.
🦠
Critical Fact: Viruses Are NOT Classified Into Any Kingdom!
When the five-kingdom system was created, no one could find a place for viruses because they don't show the normal features of living things (respiration, nutrition, reproduction on their own). Some taxonomists suggest placing viruses in a sixth kingdom, but this remains controversial.
🧬 Virus Structure 病毒结构

All viruses have TWO basic components:

  1. Genetic material 遗传物质: Either DNA or RNA (never both!) — always single-stranded
  2. Protein coat (capsid) 蛋白质衣壳: Protects the genetic material, made of protein subunits

Many viruses also have spikes 刺突 on the surface that help attach to host cells.

⚡ Why Viruses Are Not "Alive"

Viruses FAIL the RINGER test:

  • ❌ No respiration (no metabolism)
  • ❌ No nutrition (cannot make or take in food)
  • ❌ No growth (don't increase in size)
  • ❌ Cannot reproduce independently
  • ❌ No response to stimuli alone
  • ❌ No cytoplasm 细胞质, no organelles 细胞器, no cell membrane of their own
🔄 How Viruses Reproduce (Inside Host Cells Only!)

Viruses are obligate parasites （专性寄生物） — they MUST be inside a living cell to function:

  1. Virus attaches to a specific host cell (using spikes)
  2. Injects its genetic material INTO the host cell
  3. The host cell's machinery is hijacked to make NEW virus particles
  4. New viruses burst out of the host cell (often killing it)
  5. New viruses go on to infect more cells

This is why viruses cause disease — they damage or destroy the cells they infect. Examples: Flu virus, HIV, Coronavirus, Tobacco Mosaic Virus (infects plants).

📝
Exam Reminder
If asked to classify a virus into one of the five kingdoms, the answer is: It cannot be placed into any kingdom because it does not show the characteristics of living organisms. Always explain WHY (no independent metabolism, no cell structure, requires a host).

2Why cannot viruses be classified into any of the five kingdoms? Give at least TWO reasons.
Think about what makes something "alive"...
✅ Answer · 答案
Viruses cannot be placed in any kingdom because they lack key features of life:
  1. No cellular structure: they have no cytoplasm, no organelles, no cell membrane — only genetic material + protein coat.
  2. No independent metabolism: they cannot respire, feed, or excrete on their own.
  3. Cannot reproduce alone: they are obligate parasites — must hijack a host cell's machinery to make new viruses.
  4. No growth: they do not increase in size or dry mass.
Therefore they fail the RINGER test for living organisms.
病毒不能归入任何界，因为它们缺乏生命的关键特征：
  1. 无细胞结构：没有细胞质、细胞器、细胞膜——只有遗传物质+蛋白质衣壳。
  2. 无独立代谢：不能自主呼吸、摄取营养或排泄。
  3. 不能独立繁殖：是专性寄生物——必须劫持宿主细胞的机制来制造新病毒。
  4. 不生长：体积和干重不会增加。
因此它们无法通过 RINGER 生命特征测试。

📝 Grammar Points · 语法要点
passive voice: cannot be placed 被动语态——"cannot be placed in any kingdom" = 不能被放入任何界。结构：modal + be + past participle。
because + clause (reason) because 引导原因状语从句——"because they lack key features"。because 后接完整句子，说明原因。
parallel structure in lists 并列结构——四个要点都用 "No + noun" 或 "Cannot + verb" 开头，保持平行。平行结构使列举清晰有力，是学术写作的基本要求。
em-dash (—) for emphasis 破折号强调补充——"no cell membrane — only genetic material"。破折号后的内容是对前面的补充说明。
3A student discovers an unknown organism. It has a nucleus, is multicellular, absorbs nutrients from dead matter, and has chitin cell walls. Which kingdom does it belong to?
Look at the key differences between fungi and plants carefully.
✅ Answer · 答案
Kingdom: Fungi.
Reasoning: It has a nucleus (so NOT Prokaryote), is multicellular (rules out most Protoctists), and crucially has chitin cell walls + absorbs nutrients externally — both are defining features of fungi. Plants have cellulose cell walls and photosynthesise; fungi have chitin walls and absorb food from dead matter.
答案：真菌界 (Fungi)。
推理：有细胞核（排除原核生物），多细胞（排除大多数原生生物），关键是有几丁质细胞壁+从死亡物质外部吸收营养——这两点都是真菌的 defining features。植物有纤维素细胞壁并光合作用；真菌有几丁质细胞壁并吸收死亡有机物。

6Compare reptiles and amphibians. Give THREE ways they are different and ONE way they are similar.
Consider skin, eggs, habitat, breathing...
✅ Answer · 答案
Differences (3):
  1. Skin: Reptiles have dry scaly skin (prevents water loss); amphibians have moist smooth skin (used for breathing).
  2. Eggs: Reptiles lay eggs with leathery shells on land; amphibians lay jelly-like eggs in water (no shell).
  3. Breathing: Reptiles breathe with lungs only; amphibians use lungs AND skin (cutaneous respiration).
Similarity (1): Both are cold-blooded (ectothermic) — their body temperature depends on the environment.
差异（3点）：
  1. 皮肤：爬行动物有干燥鳞片皮肤（防失水）；两栖动物皮肤湿润光滑（用于呼吸）。
  2. 卵：爬行动物在陆地产革质卵；两栖动物在水中产胶状卵（无壳）。
  3. 呼吸：爬行动物仅用肺呼吸；两栖动物用肺+皮肤呼吸。
相同点（1点）：两者都是变温动物（冷血）——体温依赖环境温度。

7What is the difference between excretion and egestion? Why is this distinction important in biology?
One comes FROM metabolism, the other does NOT.
✅ Answer · 答案
Excretion: Removal of waste products produced by metabolism (chemical reactions in cells) — e.g., CO₂ from respiration, urea from protein breakdown.
Egestion: Removal of undigested food from the gut as faeces — never entered cells or metabolism.
Why important: They are fundamentally different processes. Excretion is essential to remove toxic metabolic by-products that would poison cells if accumulated. Egestion simply removes material that passed through the digestive system unchanged. Confusing them loses marks in exams!
排泄 (Excretion)：排出代谢产生的废物（细胞内化学反应的产物）——如呼吸产生的 CO₂、蛋白质分解产生的尿素。
排遗 (Egestion)：排出未消化的食物形成粪便——这些物质从未进入细胞或参与代谢。
为何重要：两者本质不同。排泄是为了移除会毒害细胞的代谢副产物；排遗只是排出通过消化系统未被吸收的物质。考试中混淆二者会失分！
📖 Language Notes · 语言解析
🔑 Key Vocabulary · 核心词汇

accumulatedv.-ed积累 accumulate = 逐渐堆积
unchangedadj.未改变的 un-（否定）+ change + -d
📝 Grammar Points · 语法要点
noun + of + noun (definition) "Removal of waste products" = 废物的排出。of 表示所属关系，书面语中常用名词短语代替动词。

8Define "species". Why are horses and donkeys considered different species even though they can mate?
The key word is "fertile"...
✅ Answer · 答案
A species is a group of organisms that can reproduce to produce fertile offspring.
Horses and donkeys can mate, but their offspring (mules) are sterile — mules cannot reproduce. Therefore horses and donkeys are different species. The word "fertile" is essential in the definition.
物种是一群能够相互交配并产生可育后代的生物。
马和驴可以交配，但后代（骡子）不育——骡子不能繁殖。因此马和驴是不同物种。定义中"可育"这个词是关键。

9An arthropod has 8 legs, no antennae, and two body parts. Which group does it belong to? Name one example.

10Humans share ~98.8% DNA with chimpanzees but only ~60% with fruit flies. Explain what this tells us about evolutionary relationships.
Think about common ancestors...
✅ Answer · 答案
Organisms that share a more recent common ancestor have DNA base sequences that are more similar. Humans and chimpanzees share 98.8% DNA → they share a recent common ancestor (~6 million years ago) and are closely related. Humans and fruit flies share only 60% DNA → they share only a distant common ancestor (~600 million years ago) and are distantly related. DNA similarity is used as a means of classification, reflecting evolutionary relationships.
共享更近共同祖先的生物，其 DNA 碱基序列更相似。人类与黑猩猩共享 98.8% DNA → 共享较近的共同祖先（约 600 万年前），亲缘关系近。人类与果蝇仅共享 60% DNA → 只共享遥远的共同祖先（约 6 亿年前），亲缘关系远。DNA 相似度作为分类依据，反映进化关系。

🔬 9.1 What Exactly Is a "Species"? 物种的严格定义 Syllabus 1.2.2

Earlier we defined a species loosely as "one type of organism". But IGCSE requires a much more precise definition:

⭐ IGCSE Official Definition

A species is a group of organisms that can reproduce to produce fertile offspring.

物种：能够相互交配并产生可育后代的一群生物

Key word: FERTILE （可育的） — the offspring must themselves be able to reproduce. This is what separates true species from look-alikes.

✅ Same Species Example

Horse + Donkey → Mule.
But wait! Mules are sterile (cannot reproduce). So horses and donkeys are different species, even though they can mate.

❌ Trap Example

Two different dog breeds (e.g. Chihuahua + Great Dane) → still produces fertile puppies. So all domestic dogs = ONE species (Canis familiaris), despite huge appearance differences.

💡
Exam Tip
If asked "Define species", always include the word fertile. Missing this word = lost marks. Many students forget it!
🧬 9.2 DNA & Evolutionary Relationships DNA 与进化关系 Syllabus 1.2.5–1.2.7

Classification isn't just about appearance — it aims to reflect evolutionary relationships （进化关系）, i.e. how closely related organisms are by common ancestry.

🔑 Three Key Principles:

  1. Classification reflects evolutionary relationships — organisms grouped together share a closer common ancestor.
  2. DNA base sequences are used as a means of classification — the sequence of A, T, G, C bases in DNA is like a "biological barcode" unique to each species.
  3. More recent common ancestor = more similar DNA — organisms that share a recent ancestor have base sequences that are more similar than those sharing only a distant ancestor.

Worked example — comparing primates:

Pair of organisms% DNA similarityCommon ancestorRelationship
Human ↔ Chimpanzee~98.8%Recent (~6 million years ago)Very close 🟢
Human ↔ Mouse~85%Distant (~90 million years ago)Distant 🟡

Memory hook: Closer on the evolutionary tree = more similar DNA. Think of DNA as a "family history book" — close relatives have nearly identical books; distant cousins have only a few pages in common.
🦗 9.3 Arthropods — The Four Groups 节肢动物四大类 Syllabus 1.3.2

Arthropods （节肢动物） are the largest animal group — they have jointed legs （分节的足） and a hard exoskeleton （外骨骼） made of chitin. IGCSE requires you to know the four main groups based on body features and number of legs:

🐛

Myriapods 多足类

e.g., Centipedes, Millipedes
  • Many legs — one or two pairs per body segment
  • Long, segmented body （分节身体）
  • One pair of antennae （触角）
  • Centipedes: carnivorous, 1 pair legs/segment
  • Millipedes: herbivorous, 2 pairs legs/segment
  • Live in damp places (under logs, leaves)
🐝

Insects 昆虫

e.g., Bees, Flies, Butterflies, Ants
  • Three body parts: head, thorax, abdomen
  • Six legs (3 pairs) — attached to thorax
  • One or two pairs of wings （翅）
  • One pair of antennae
  • Breathe through spiracles （气门）
  • Largest arthropod group — over 1 million species!
🕷️

Arachnids 蛛形类

e.g., Spiders, Scorpions, Ticks, Mites
  • Eight legs (4 pairs) — not 6 like insects!
  • Two body parts: cephalothorax + abdomen
  • No antennae (distinguishes them from insects)
  • No wings
  • Usually carnivorous — spiders spin webs using silk glands
  • Four pairs of simple eyes (not compound)
🦀

Crustaceans 甲壳类

e.g., Crabs, Lobsters, Woodlice, Shrimp
  • Usually more than 8 legs (often 10+ pairs)
  • Two pairs of antennae (unique feature!)
  • Body often covered by a hard calcium carbonate shell
  • Most live in water (marine/freshwater), some on land (woodlice)
  • Breathe through gills (even land ones!)
  • Five or more pairs of limbs

Quick arthropod ID key:
Has 6 legs? → Insect (check for wings + 3 body parts)
Has 8 legs + no antennae? → Arachnid
Has 2 pairs of antennae? → Crustacean
